===Etymology===
From {{inh|en|enm|also}}, {{m|enm|alswo}}, {{m|enm|alswa}} (also {{m|enm|als|als(e)}}, {{m|enm|as}}, whence English {{m|en|as}}), from {{inh|en|ang|ealswā||just like, also}}, from {{inh|en|gmw-pro|*allswā}}, equivalent to {{com|en|all|so}}. Cognate with {{cog|sco|alsa}}, {{m|sco|alswa||also, even so, in the same way, as, as well}}, {{cog|stq|also||accordingly, therefore, thus}}, {{cog|fy|alsa||so, just so, even so, thus}}, {{cog|osx|alsō||similarly, as if, just as, when}}, {{cog|nl|alzo||so, thus}}, {{cog|de|also||so, thus}}, {{cog|da|altså||so}}, {{cog|nb|altså||so, therefore, accordingly, thus}}, {{cog|nn|altso||so, accordingly, therefore, thus}}, {{cog|sv|alltså||so, therefore, accordingly, thus, then}}. {{doublet|en|as}}. More at {{m|en|all}}, {{m|en|so}}.

====Usage notes====
The adverb '''also''' can go in various places within a sentence, with minimal change of meaning.
*{{ux|en|The golf courses are excellent. '''Also''', the tennis courts are fantastic.}}
*{{ux|en|The golf courses are excellent and the tennis courts are fantastic '''also'''.}}
*{{ux|en|Not only are the golf courses excellent but '''also''' the tennis courts are fantastic.}}
*{{ux|en|There are not just excellent golf courses here. There are '''also''' fantastic tennis courts.}}
